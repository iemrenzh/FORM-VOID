STUDIO PHILOSOPHY

The Poetics of
Inhabited Space

Architecture is the art of organizing silence. Every structural decision is a choreography of light, shadow, material, and void — each element speaking to the human condition.

At FORM + VOID, we pursue what we call "Structural Empathy" — the discipline of designing spaces that respond to how people truly move, feel, and live.

THE STUDIO

Built on
Conviction.

Founded in 2007 by Elena Vasquez and Marcus Cole, FORM + VOID is an architectural syndicate committed to the idea that buildings should be felt, not just seen.

Over seventeen years, we have completed 183 projects across 17 countries, earning international recognition for our commitment to structural integrity, material honesty, and spatial empathy.

Our
Values
01

Material Honesty

We use materials as they are meant to be used — concrete poured, timber grown, glass floated. No veneers. No deception. Every surface tells the truth of its making.

02

Structural Empathy

We design by inhabiting the space imaginatively before the first drawing. What will the morning light do? How will the sound travel? What does this threshold mean?

03

Ecological Responsibility

Every project must pass our ecological audit. We design for a 200-year life span with minimal operational energy and maximum structural adaptability.

04

Long-View Collaboration

We build relationships as carefully as we build buildings. Many of our clients return for second and third projects. That continuity deepens the work.

IGLIBE
FORM + VOID — MANIFESTO

The
Case for
Silence.

We live in an age of architectural noise. Buildings compete for attention like billboards — each one louder, stranger, more adorned than the last. We believe this is a misreading of what architecture is for.

"The greatest buildings are those that make you feel more completely yourself."

Architecture's first duty is not to impress. It is to shelter — in the deepest sense of that word. To shelter the body, yes. But also the mind, the spirit, the private self that needs respite from the relentless demand of the modern world.

We practice what we call Structural Empathy: the discipline of designing from the inside out, beginning always with the question of how a space will be lived before we ask how it will be seen. This is not sentimentalism. It is precision of a different order.

We believe in the intelligence of materials. Concrete cast in place holds the memory of its forming — the grain of the wood, the seams of the boards. Glass floated over molten tin achieves a clarity that no subsequent treatment can replicate. Timber, properly detailed, breathes with the seasons. We use materials as they wish to be used, and in doing so, we align ourselves with forces larger than our own ambitions.

"To build well is to listen — to the site, the client, the materials, the light."

We are not minimalists, though our work is often quiet. We are not maximalists, though our spaces are often rich. We are structuralists — people who believe that the right organization of space, material, and light can produce something close to the experience of grace.

This is what we build toward. Not awards. Not accolades. Not the appearance of innovation. We build toward the moment when someone walks through a door we have designed and, without quite knowing why, feels that they have arrived somewhere that has been waiting for them.

— ELENA VASQUEZ + MARCUS COLE, FORM + VOID, 2024
